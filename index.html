08

Outbound results

Outbound calls only, even in this view
Every outbound dial — connected, failed and initiated — deduplicated by Call ID. Reach and connectivity come from all dials; lead quality comes from the calls that connected.

Outbound conversion funnel

Dials placed → connected → hot leads. The red row is the failed-call leakage — dials that never connected. Click any bar (or the leakage) for the calls behind it.

Reach & disposition

Every outbound dial by outcome. Initiated = a dial logged with no terminal status (didn't connect).

Repeatedly unreachable

Numbers dialed 3+ times with no recorded connection. Review before the next retry or move them to another channel.

Retry effectiveness

Use this to set a sensible retry cap. It shows where leads first connected across their outbound dial attempts in the selected period. First successful connect = share of reached leads whose first connection happened on that attempt; this is operational call data, not an AI score or forecast.

Outbound calling playbook

Fresh lead: call immediately. Retries: use the best all-days 2-hour window. Meaningful = 60+ seconds.
View day & time detail
Supporting evidence only — do not delay a fresh lead to wait for a specific day.

Dial timing vs yield

Are dials going into the windows that actually connect and convert? Volume, connect rate, and hot-lead rate side by side — by hour block or weekday.
Outbound Performance & Conversion is hidden in Inbound view — connect rate, reach, and redial economics aren't meaningful for calls the lead initiated themselves. It reappears under Outbound or All calls.
09

Outbound Cadence & Capacity

Outbound calls only, even in this view
How many lines you need, whether the dialer is honouring your calling policy, and the retry cadence that actually gets a connection without over-calling.

Concurrency & capacity

Simultaneous live conversations across the selected range — peak overlap sets your minimum channel/licence count. Talk time only; a ringing line is also occupied, so treat the peak as a floor.
Peak concurrent calls by hour of day (IST)

Calling-policy compliance

Your rule: after a no-pickup, up to 3 more attempts at ~5-hour intervals, only within 9am–9pm. Whether the dialer is actually doing that.

Retry economics — what cadence actually works

The right policy read off your own data: how many attempts you actually need, and whether the 5-hour gap earns its keep.
Cumulative reach by attempt # (of everyone ever reached)
Connect rate of a retry, by gap since last try
Outbound Cadence & Capacity is hidden in Inbound view — dial cadence, retry policy, and connect economics only apply to calls you place. It reappears under Outbound or All calls.
